Workspaces is the organizational layer for a project. It's a single place to gather everything relevant to the project: other projects, external data sources, shared documentation, and team members.

This hub for a project's entire body of work helps individuals execute their work more effectively, while keeping teams in-sync throughout the game's development pipeline.

## What a Workspace contains

Everything below belongs to the Workspace. Meaning that you can work on multiple projects in a single Workspace, and all the information below will remain available.

### Connections

Multiple Unity Projects and external local folders can live in a Workspace. _In a Team Workspace, your Connections are private and not visible or accessible by others in the Team._

> [Connections documentation](https://docs.bezi.com/context/connections)

### Shared and Private Pages

Pages can hold documentation, specs, coding standards, and architecture references. They provide context across everything and everyone in a Workspace. Private Pages mean they're only visible and act as context for you.

Shared Pages are visible to all team members in the Workspace and are automatically incorporated in everyone's context (only available on Team and Enterprise plans).

> [Pages documentation](https://docs.bezi.com/context/pages)

### Plans

Plans are private to you. To share a great Plan with someone in your Team Workspace, convert it to a Page and promote it to a Shared Page.

> [Plan Mode documentation](https://docs.bezi.com/fundamentals/plan-mode)

### Rules

Rules are private to you and apply to all threads and Connections in a Workspace. For Team Workspaces that want standardized Rules, define them in a Shared Page for everyone to copy.

> [Rules documentation](https://docs.bezi.com/fundamentals/rules)

### Threads

At the start of each thread you'll declare a Target Project to inform Bezi where to focus. Create a new thread if for each target project and for separate tasks within each.

> [Threads documentation](https://docs.bezi.com/fundamentals/threads)

## Managing Workspaces

### Create a new Workspace

1. Open the **Workspace dropdown**, located at the top of the left sidebar
2. Click `New Workspace`
3. To tailor or manage it, open **Workspace dropdown** \> `Workspace Settings`

### Switch between Workspaces

1. Open the **Workspace dropdown**, located at the top of the left sidebar
2. Select the other Workspace you want to open from the list
