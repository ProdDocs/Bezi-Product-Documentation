## Pen tool&#x20;

The Pen tool is used to draw shapes on 2D planes.

<Accordion title="Using the Pen tool" defaultOpen={false}>
  1. Press `p` + `spacebar` activate the Pen too&#x6C;**.** The Pen tool can also be accessed under the **Draw** menu in the top toolbar

  2. Once active, click to place a point in the scene and begin to draw. Repeat to draw a shape

  3. Click the first point to close the shape
</Accordion>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Editing the curve" defaultOpen={false}>
    To edit the curve, double click on the curve and the points will appear. Click and drag the points and curve handles around to adjust.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Fill or extrude the curve" defaultOpen={false}>
    1. Select the curve in the scene

    2. Navigate to the 'Planar Curve' section of the Properties Panel&#x20;

    3. Adjust the Fill style and extrude the curve to create 3D geometry
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pipe the curve" defaultOpen={false}>
    1. Select the curve in the scene

    2. Navigate to the 'Planar Curve' section of the Properties Panel&#x20;

    3. Adjust the Pipe style to create geometry along the curve
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Line tool

The Line tool is used to draw shapes in a 3D space.

<Accordion title="Using the Line tool" defaultOpen={false}>
  1. Press `ln` + `spacebar` activate the Line tool. The Linr tool can also be accessed under the **Draw** menu in the top toolbar

  2. Once active, click to place a point in the scene and begin to draw. Repeat to draw a shape

  3. Click the first point to close the shape
</Accordion>

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Editing the shape" defaultOpen={false}>
    To edit the line, double click on the curve and the points will appear. Click and drag the points around to adjust.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pipe the curve" defaultOpen={false}>
    1. Select the curve in the scene

    2. Navigate to the '3D Curve' section of the Properties Panel&#x20;

    3. Adjust the Pipe style to create geometry along the curve
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
